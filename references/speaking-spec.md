# Speaking Module Specification

## Overview
- Total duration: 11–14 minutes
- Marking: Fluency & Coherence · Lexical Resource · Grammatical Range & Accuracy · Pronunciation
- Platform: Recorded response — students record themselves and receive feedback
- Model answers required: Band 6, Band 7, Band 8 transcripts for every Part 2 cue card

---

## Part 1 — Introduction & Interview

| Parameter | Specification |
|---|---|
| Duration | 4–5 minutes |
| Format | Examiner asks questions about familiar topics |
| Topics | Home · family · work/study · hobbies · daily routine · food · travel · technology |
| Questions per topic | 3–4 |
| Number of topics | 2–3 |

**Question writing rules:**
- Personal and accessible — no research or specialist knowledge required
- Mix of: simple questions ("Do you enjoy cooking?") + follow-up probes ("Why is that?") + preference questions ("Which do you prefer?")
- Never ask questions that could embarrass or distress a student
- Avoid yes/no dead-ends — always follow up with "Why?" or "Tell me more"

---

## Part 2 — Individual Long Turn (Cue Card)

| Parameter | Specification |
|---|---|
| Preparation Time | 1 minute (student makes notes — recording NOT active) |
| Speaking Time | 1–2 minutes |
| Format | Cue card with topic + 3–4 bullet-point prompts |
| Follow-Up | 1–2 short questions from examiner after the talk |

**Standard cue card template:**
```
Describe a [person / place / object / experience / time] that [was important / impressed you / you remember well].

You should say:
• what / who it was
• when and where it happened / you encountered it
• what happened / what made it special
• and explain why it was important / why you remember it so clearly
```

**Cue card writing rules:**
- Topic must be personal and concrete
- 3–4 bullet prompts must cover: What/Who · When/Where · How/Why · Personal reflection
- Topic must be speakable by any student regardless of background
- Follow-up questions must connect directly to the cue card topic

---

## Part 3 — Two-Way Discussion

| Parameter | Specification |
|---|---|
| Duration | 4–5 minutes |
| Format | Abstract discussion linked thematically to Part 2 |
| Depth | Analytical — opinions, comparisons, speculation |
| Questions | 5–7, escalating in abstraction |

**Question writing rules:**
- Thematically connected to the Part 2 cue card
- Escalate: concrete → abstract → speculative
- Include: opinion questions + comparison questions + cause/effect questions + at least one hypothetical
- Use: "To what extent...," "How far do you think...," "What might be the long-term consequences of...?"
- No question answerable with yes or no — flag and rewrite any that are

## Band Descriptors — Speaking

| Band | Fluency | Vocabulary | Grammar | Pronunciation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 8–9 | Effortless; no noticeable hesitation | Precise, flexible, idiomatic | Wide range; errors are slips only | Clear at all times |
| 7 | Generally fluent; occasional hesitation | Good range; some imprecision | Good range; some errors | Generally clear; minor impact |
| 6 | Some hesitation but ideas develop | Adequate range; some repetition | Mix of structures; errors present | Understandable; some strain |
| 5 | Noticeable hesitation; ideas not always clear | Limited range; frequent repetition | Mainly simple structures | Noticeable difficulty at times |

**Model answer requirements:**
- Band 6, Band 7, Band 8 sample transcript for every Part 2 cue card
- Annotate each: highlight strong vocabulary, good grammar, weak points
- Include timing note: "This answer runs approximately 90 seconds at Band 7 pace"

---

## QA Checklist — Run Before Output

- [ ] Part 1 has 2–3 topics with 3–4 questions each
- [ ] Part 1 questions are personal, accessible, and open-ended
- [ ] Part 2 cue card follows the standard template (What / When / How / Personal reflection)
- [ ] Part 2 follow-up questions connect directly to the cue card topic
- [ ] Part 3 questions are thematically linked to Part 2
- [ ] Part 3 escalates from concrete to abstract to speculative
- [ ] Part 3 includes at least one "To what extent..." or hypothetical question
- [ ] No Part 3 question can be answered yes/no
- [ ] Three model transcripts provided (Band 6, Band 7, Band 8) for Part 2
- [ ] Each transcript is annotated with examiner-style commentary
- [ ] Each transcript includes a timing note
